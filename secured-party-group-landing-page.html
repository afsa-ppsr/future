Secured party groups

A secured party group (SPG) is different to your PPSR account. You will need to create both an account and a SPG before registering a security interest.

A SPG contains the details of the person or organisation who has an interest in the propert - such as yourself or your organisation.

What is a secured party?

A Secured Party is the seller, lessor, lender, consignor, financier or other entity that has a legal claim to personal property registered on the PPSR.

What is a secured party group?

A secured party group or an SPG refers to one or more secured parties that have a security interest in the same personal property.

Example scenario

Printernational Pty Ltd supplies office equipment. Printernational is the secured party. Page and Co leases a printer from Printernational. Printernational make a registration over the collateral office equipment and list Page and Co as the grantor. If Page and Co is unable to pay or goes broke, Printernational Pty Ltd will be able to make a claim to get their printer and any other office equipment they supplied back. To read more about how Printernational created their registration, visit Commercial lease of office equipment.
