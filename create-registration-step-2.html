Additional details

Select the details that apply.

If you check a box, you are answering 'yes'.

Click + for more information.

+

Check this box only if you have a purchase money security (PMSI). You probably have a PMSI if you’ve lent money or provided credit for the purpose of funding all or part of the purchase price of the personal property. A PMSI gives you first right to enforce against the property (“super priority”) even if there are earlier registered security interests that would cover it.

Examples of PMSIs include supply of goods on retention of title terms, PPS Leases or secured loans for the purchase of specified personal property (like a car loan).

If you have a PMSI you must register within the correct time frame and check the PMSI box. If you don’t, you won’t get PMSI super priority.

More information about PMSI's can be found on the PPSR Website.

Note: It is very important to get this right as there can be serious consequences for getting it wrong. For example, if you check the box saying your registration is a PMSI and it is not, your registration may be ineffective. If in doubt, please seek professional advice.

+

Checking this box indicates that the collateral you have an interest in includes inventory. Inventory is goods in the possession of your business that are being offered or made ready for sale. It could be your floor stock or raw materials that you’re going to use to make finished goods.

If your collateral is inventory, it may be classed as a 'circulating asset'. This may be important when it comes to enforcing your security interest and for the purposes of the Corporations Act 2010 (Cth).

+

Whether or not assets are subject to control depends upon various things such as whether:

  • the property is in your possession,
  • the grantor has acquired property that cannot be removed without your permission or
  • you manage funds deposited with you in an account.

This may be important when it comes to enforcing your security interest and for the purposes of the Corporations Act 2010 (Cth).

+

Checking this box will indicate that your registration ranks lower than another registration. Usually, this option is ticked at a later date to amend the registration because you have agreed to allow another registration to rank ahead of yours (even though yours would normally have priority). You can check this box at any point after you register if needed.

+

Claiming proceeds is important if someone sells or disposes of the property you have a registration over. Your security interest will usually extend to any proceeds that come about from the disposal of the collateral. This includes things like:

  • money made from its sale
  • insurance payouts
  • compensation payments on the property
  • any indirect dealing with the property

For example, if you have a security interest in a car that is sold by the grantor, the sale price can be proceeds. If the car is destroyed, an insurance payout can be proceeds too.

You will see that the ‘Proceeds to be claimed’ box is checked by default and contains the text 'All present and after acquired property'. This means you claim an interest in all of the current and future proceeds of the collateral.

Giving of notice identifier

This field lets you enter a reference for your registration (such as a customer reference number or customer/client ID).

It is entirely up to you as to what you want to include in this field. Anyone who sends you correspondence about your registration must include the GONI.

Earlier registration number

This optional field allows you to enter the PPSR registration number of an earlier registration which is related to your new registration. This is relevant if you want the new registration to maintain the priority position of the earlier one.

You can only use this to enter a current PPSR registration, not a discharged, expired or removed registration, so you must make the new one first.
